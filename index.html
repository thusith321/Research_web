Project Scope

Literature Survey


Respiratory diseases pose a significant global health challenge, necessitating the development of precise, non-invasive diagnostic tools. This thesis explores the integration of explainable artificial intelligence (AI) in 1ioacoustics and X-ray analysis to enhance respiratory disease diagnostics. The focus is on developing and optimizing hardware and software systems to capture 1ioacoustics signals from the human body, aiming to improve the sensitivity and comfort of sensors, ensure long-term stability and performance, consider user acceptance, and implement robust privacy measures. Additionally, designing cost-effective solutions is crucial to making these advanced diagnostic technologies accessible to a broader population

The research investigates two innovative methods for detecting breath sounds, the MicrotoneStethoscope and the Dual Rhythm Stethoscope. The MicrotoneStethoscope utilizes an ESP32 microcontroller, an INMP441 omnidirectional microphone, clips, power cables, jumper wires, clip headers, and a switch. Data is captured using the Arduino IDE, transmitted to an AWS database, and converted to MP3 format using WinSCP. Conversely, the Dual Rhythm Stethoscope incorporates a microphone connected to a stethoscope via a 3.5 mm jack cable, with data captured directly and sent to a mobile application for analysis.

The primary goal is to differentiate between healthy and unhealthy individuals based on breath sounds captured by these two methods. By leveraging these devices, the research demonstrates the feasibility of using 1ioacoustics data for accurate respiratory health diagnostics. In the pursuit of improving 1ioacoustics signal capture technology, this thesis emphasizes enhancing sensor sensitivity and user comfort to capture a comprehensive range of signals 2 non-invasively. Ensuring the stability and performance of hardware and software components over extended periods is essential for reliable long-term monitoring. User acceptance is also crucial, necessitating privacy measures to protect sensitive health data and addressing concerns about data security and ethical usage. Furthermore, the development of cost-effective solutions is imperative to ensure that these advanced technologies are accessible to a wider population, particularly in resource-limited settings

By achieving these objectives, this research contributes significantly to the field of respiratory diagnostics, providing a robust framework for future advancements in 2ioacoustics signal capture and analysis. The integration of explainable AI in this context not only enhances diagnostic accuracy but also ensures transparency and trust in the technology, paving the way for broader adoption in clinical settings. This work represents a step forward in leveraging modern technological advancements to address pressing healthcare challenges, ultimately improving patient outcomes and advancing public health

Research Problem & Solution

Proposed Problem

Our biggest challenge

Respiratory diseases, such as chronic obstructive pulmonary disease, require precise, non-invasive diagnostic tools for early detection and effective management. Traditional diagnostic methods, including stethoscope auscultation and X-ray imaging, often necessitate expert interpretation and can be invasive or uncomfortable for patients, limiting their suitability for continuous monitoring and early diagnosis. Current bioacoustic signal capture technologies present an alternative but face significant challenges, including a compromise between sensor sensitivity and comfort, performance degradation over time, user discomfort, privacy concerns, and high costs, which restrict their widespread adoption and effectiveness

Proposed Solution

This research proposes developing and optimizing hardware and software systems for capturing bioacoustic signals to improve the diagnosis and monitoring of respiratory diseases. The focus is on enhancing sensor sensitivity to detect subtle respiratory sounds while ensuring comfort for long-term use. Stability and performance of the hardware and software over extended periods will be prioritized to enable reliable continuous monitoring. To increase user acceptance, the design will incorporate user-friendly features and robust privacy measures. Additionally, the research aims to create cost-effective solutions, making advanced respiratory diagnostics accessible to a broader population. Utilizing explainable AI will further enhance diagnostic accuracy and transparency, ultimately improving patient outcomes and public health.

Research Objectives

Improve sensor sensitivity and comfort to accurately capture a wide range of bioacoustic signals non-invasively​ ​.

Enhance sensor sensitivity and comfort to accurately and non-invasively capture a broad spectrum of bioacoustic signals. This advancement aims to facilitate early diagnosis and continuous monitoring of respiratory diseases by ensuring high-fidelity data collection without compromising patient comfort. By addressing the balance between sensitivity and usability, the goal is to improve the practicality and effectiveness of bioacoustic monitoring devices, enabling reliable long-term health management and better patient outcomes..

Leveraging Audio Spectrogram Transformer (AST) for Advanced Respiratory Sound Analysis.

Adopt AST for enhanced pattern recognition in spectrogram-based audio data, leveraging the transformer architecture's ability to handle sequential data. This makes it ideal for capturing the temporal dynamics of respiratory sounds. By effectively analyzing these patterns, AST ensures precise, non-invasive diagnostics, facilitating early detection and continuous monitoring of respiratory diseases. This approach promises significant improvements in diagnostic accuracy and patient outcomes through advanced audio data analysis..

Patch-Mix Contrastive Learning for Enhanced Respiratory Disease Classification.

A novel approach to refine the model's ability to distinguish between disease-specific sound signatures. Patch-Mix Contrastive Learning enhances latent space representations by contrasting mixed audio samples. This leads to improved classification accuracy, allowing for more precise identification of respiratory conditions. By effectively differentiating between subtle variations in respiratory sounds, this method significantly enhances diagnostic capabilities and supports continuous monitoring, ultimately improving patient outcomes and healthcare efficiency.

Vision-Language Model for Automated Chest X-ray Abnormality Detection.

A core AI technology facilitating automatic detection of abnormalities in chest X-rays, generating detailed notes on identified issues' type and location. This model enhances diagnostic accuracy and efficiency, streamlining clinical workflows and improving patient care by providing comprehensive, automated X-ray analysis.

Methodology

Figure 2. High Level Architecture of the system.

The proposed system consists of 3 main components. They are;

  1. Developing and optimizing of the hardware and software needed to capture bioacoustics signals
  2. Advancing Lung Sound Classification: Novel Audio Augmentation in Transformer Latent Space​
  3. Implementing AI in Respiratory X-Ray Diagnostics

The hardware component of bioacoustic signal capture systems has seen substantial advancements, particularly with the advent of microcontrollers and high-fidelity microphones. The ESP32 microcontroller, known for its low power consumption and integrated Wi-Fi and Bluetooth capabilities, has been widely adopted in IoT applications, including medical diagnostics. The INMP441 omnidirectional microphone, noted for its high signal-to-noise ratio and low distortion, has been utilized in various bioacoustic applications. Research by D. S. Kumar et al. (2019) demonstrated its efficacy in capturing high-quality respiratory sounds, crucial for accurate diagnostics.

The stability and performance of hardware and software over extended monitoring periods are vital for reliable diagnostics. Continuous monitoring systems must be robust against environmental variations and user-induced noise. Studies by H. L. Chang et al. (2021) focused on enhancing the durability and accuracy of wearable bioacoustic sensors through advanced materials and adaptive algorithms. 5 Software stability is equally important. The integration of real-time data processing and cloud storage, as demonstrated in systems using AWS for data management, ensures that diagnostic data is both secure and accessible. The Arduino IDE’s role in facilitating rapid prototyping and deployment of bioacoustic capture systems has been extensively documented in educational and professional research, emphasizing its utility in developing stable and scalable solutions.
